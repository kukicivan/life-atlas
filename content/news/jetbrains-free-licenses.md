> Every JetBrains IDE. Free. Legally.

## Two Ways to Get It

### 1. Students & Teachers

If you have a university email or student ID:

1. Go to [jetbrains.com/community/education](https://www.jetbrains.com/community/education/#students)
2. Apply with your .edu email (or upload student ID)
3. Get approved (usually within minutes)
4. Download any IDE — IntelliJ IDEA Ultimate, WebStorm, PyCharm Professional, Rider, GoLand, all of them

License renews annually while you're a student.

### 2. Open Source Contributors

If you maintain an open-source project (at least 3 months active):

1. Go to [jetbrains.com/community/opensource](https://www.jetbrains.com/community/opensource/#support)
2. Submit your project details
3. Get a 1-year All Products license

Your GitHub repos count. Even small tools and utilities.

## What You're Getting

The All Products Pack normally costs **$249/year** (first year). It includes:

- **IntelliJ IDEA Ultimate** — Java, Kotlin, Spring
- **WebStorm** — JavaScript, TypeScript, React, Angular, Vue
- **PyCharm Professional** — Python, Django, FastAPI
- **Rider** — .NET, C#, Unity
- **GoLand** — Go
- **DataGrip** — Database management
- **CLion** — C/C++
- Plus all other JetBrains IDEs

## Why This Matters

VS Code is great with extensions. But JetBrains IDEs have deeper refactoring, better debugging, and built-in database tools that VS Code can't match out of the box. Having both is the move.
